All results are computed by scanning \texttt{SKILL.md} files in four repositories (\texttt{chinese\_gpt3.5\_8}, \texttt{english\_gpt3.5\_8}, \texttt{chinese\_gpt4\_8}, \texttt{english\_gpt4\_8}), yielding $N=1{,}858$ skills. Category and corpus shares are frequency-normalized percentages, i.e., \texttt{count}/1858\,$\\times$\,100, and top tags are aggregated from YAML \texttt{tags} with case-insensitive normalization for Latin tokens. Platform frequencies are keyword-based mention counts over skill metadata (name/description/tags/triggers). Across these repositories, extracted skills cluster into two dominant capability families: implementation-oriented skills (programming, data/AI, systems engineering) and communication-oriented skills (writing, translation, content production). Programming and software development is the largest category (482, 25.94%), followed by writing/content creation (363, 19.54%) and data/AI workflows (354, 19.05%), indicating that AutoSkill is increasingly capturing reusable procedural know-how for multi-step technical work rather than only stylistic preferences.
